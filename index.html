Hi!

I'm Janna, a graduate student currently pursuing an MS Physics degree at the National Institute of Physics, University of the Philippines Diliman. I am also part of the Gravity group under the direction of my adviser, Dr. Ian Vega.

My research over the past few years has focused on cosmology, though I am broadly interested in relativistic astrophysics and gravitational physics, and would like to learn more about black holes, gravitational waves, etc. During my undergraduate years, I studied the effect of dark matter-dark energy coupling on theoretical cosmological models with the use of dynamical systems. For my present Master's research, I study generalized McVittie solutions with the goal of exploring the interaction between local physics (particularly the structure of compact objects) and cosmological expansion.

Outside of physics, I mostly read. I love stories so I gobble up all kinds of literature that piques my curiosity. Although, time constraints have recently led me to favor audiobooks more. My outdoor activities include the occasional travel with my family and cycling. I also like being surrounded by nature, especially in a place with little light pollution so that I can appreciate the beauty of the night sky.
